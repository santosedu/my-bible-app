# ADR-001: Full Study MVP Product Approach

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-04-05

## Context

The Bible reader app needs a product approach that balances delivering a compelling study experience against time-to-market. The target user is a study-focused reader who needs deep Bible study tools. The app must be responsive (desktop, tablet, mobile), support dark/light theme, and have an Apple-like interface. Key decisions include which features to include in the MVP versus deferring to later phases.

## Decision

Ship a Full Study MVP that includes all core study tools in Phase 1: Bible navigation (book > chapter > verse), Portuguese translations (ARA, ACF, NVI), multi-translation side-by-side comparison, highlights & notes, cross-references, full-text search, bookmarks, reading progress tracking, dark/light theme, and responsive Apple-like UI. Reading plans, cloud sync, audio, and multi-language support are deferred to Phase 2 and 3.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Reader-first MVP

- **Description**: MVP ships with only Bible navigation, single translation reading, bookmarks, and dark/light theme. All study tools deferred to Phase 2.
- **Pros**: Smaller initial scope, faster to deliver, simpler to test.
- **Cons**: Does not serve the study-focused persona well; users would need to wait for the features that matter most to them.
- **Why rejected**: The primary persona is a study-focused reader. Shipping without study tools would not meet the core user need.

### Alternative 2: All-at-once

- **Description**: Ship everything in a single release including reading plans, audio, and multi-language support.
- **Pros**: Maximum user value at launch; no phased rollout complexity.
- **Cons**: Significantly larger scope, longer delivery time, higher risk of quality issues, harder to gather early user feedback.
- **Why rejected**: Too much scope for a first release. Phased delivery allows validating the core experience before investing in secondary features.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Study-focused users get a complete toolset from day one.
- Multi-translation comparison, highlights, notes, and search differentiate the app from simple reader apps.
- Local-first persistence means no backend dependency for MVP.
- Phased plan provides clear roadmap for growth.

### Negative

- Larger MVP scope means longer initial development time compared to a reader-first approach.
- More complex UI with study panels (comparison view, notes editor, search results) requires careful responsive design work.

### Risks

- Scope creep during Phase 1 could delay delivery — mitigated by strict YAGNI enforcement and clear Non-Goals.
- Finding quality Portuguese Bible data sources — mitigated by researching available APIs during TechSpec phase.

## Implementation Notes

Phase 1 delivers the full study experience with local persistence. Phase 2 adds reading plans and cloud sync. Phase 3 adds audio playback and additional language support.
